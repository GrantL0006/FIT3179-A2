What is Electricity Consumption?

The electricity consumption per capita is an important measure of a country's electric power consumption, it is strongly tied to the economic growth from a sustainable development perspective. The electricity consumption per capita is calculated based on the total electricity generations in the country from all sources, such as power plants, solar plants â€¦ etc., and subtracted the power losses in the distribution process such as electricity transmissions. The net electricity production in the country is divided by the mid-year population on the country, indicating the average electricity consumed by everyone. It is important to understand the energy consumption does not only reflect the amount of electricity usage by the population, but also show the development of a country. The electricity consumption is a sub-section of energy consumption, which includes not only the energy requires to live comfortably but is also includes the electricity consumptions to operate transportations and factories.

How Much are We Consuming?

The above map shows the electricity consumption per capita in the selected year, which is defaulted in 2019, the most recent data. The map offers the ability to see how each country consume electricity on per capita basis compared to the rest of the world from 2000 to 2019. The recent two decays of electricity consumption are shown as the year 2000 has a sudden increase in global electricity consumption with various methods of electricity generation. The dropdown menu in the map depicts the primary 9 methods in electricity generations, which includes electricity generation through biofuel, coal, gas, hydro, nuclear, oil, renewables, solar and wind. Through selecting these individual electricity source, the map will depict the electricity consumption per capita from that electricity production method.

How Electricity Consumption Change Over Time?

Above temporal line chart shows the overall change in total consumption per capita globally. As shown in the line chart, there is a significant increase in the electricity usage at the year of 2000. Where electricity becomes one of the dominant forms of energy and are heavily consumed globally. The stacked area chart shown above shows the breakdown of different electricity generation methods. Where the area depicts the contribution of each source over time to the total energy consumption. The line chart offers the interactivity to manually select a year interval on the chart where the stacked area chart would zoom in to the selected year interval, allowing for a closer analysis. Furthermore, the legend also allows to highlight the electricity source you are interested which will reflect in the stacked area chart.

From the stacked area chart, it depicts that the renewable energy has been the dominant electricity source since 1985 and continues to be one of the primary forms of electricity generation method. Some of the most recent green energy source such as solar and wind electricity generation only become viable generation methods in the recent years. This is reflected in the area chart where they only gradually to make some noticeable contributions starting from around 2010. Hydro and nuclear electricity consumption has been consistent through the years while coal and gas electricity generation have a gradual increase in the 2000s. Electricity generated through oil has the greatest change in the 2000s where the proportion at which electricity consumed that came from oil has increased more than 10 times compared to 1990s.
